About the Game

The Mystery Within

Explore a sealed ancestral haveli, uncover a hidden bloodline, and survive an escalating supernatural entity in this first-person psychological horror experience driven by ritual, atmosphere, and discovery.

Rahasya is a first-person psychological horror game set inside a sealed ancestral Indian haveli — a place where architecture holds memory, where shadows carry history, and where silence is never truly empty.

There are no weapons. No combat. Only observation, ritual, and the terrifying realization that you are not alone — and never were.

Told through environment, object, and whispered truth, Rahasya does not explain its horrors. It asks you to understand them.

Indian Folklore Psychological Tension Isolation Ancient Rituals Supernatural Inheritance Forgotten Histories Atmospheric Dread Environmental Storytelling Mystery-driven Exploration Slow Escalation

The Premise

After reclaiming his ancestral home following decades of legal disputes, the protagonist begins experiencing unexplained disturbances and recurring dreams. As events escalate, he isolates himself inside the haveli to uncover the truth — only to discover a hidden legacy tied to an ancient supernatural responsibility.

Gameplay Overview

Survive Through Understanding

Rahasya is built around exploration, environmental puzzles, and atmospheric survival. Players navigate a large, interconnected haveli — unlocking new areas by solving puzzles, interpreting environmental clues, and engaging with ritual-based systems. A supernatural entity roams the halls at all times, growing more aggressive as the truth comes closer.

No Combat — Pure Psychological Horror

Rahasya contains no weapons, no combat mechanics, and no jump-scare dependency. The horror is atmospheric, environmental, and deeply personal. You cannot fight what you cannot understand.

Visual Direction

Architecture of Dread

Rahasya features photorealistic Indian haveli architecture rendered with an unflinching attention to material, shadow, and decay. Every hallway tells a story through texture alone.

The visual language is restrained by design — no exaggerated effects, no artificial horror aesthetics. The environment itself is the horror.

Low-light interiors with practical candle and lantern sources
Cinematic shadows and atmospheric fog layers
Minimal UI — full immersion without visual clutter
Subtle environmental changes that reflect supernatural escalation
Realistic materials — aged stone, weathered wood, tarnished brass
No exaggerated horror effects — grounded, cinematic realism

Frequently Asked

Questions & Answers

Rahasya is a psychological, atmospheric horror experience. There are no jump scares as the primary tool, no combat, and no gore. The horror emerges from isolation, discovery, environmental storytelling, and the escalating presence of a supernatural entity that grows more threatening as you approach the truth.

No. Rahasya contains zero combat mechanics. Survival depends entirely on movement, spatial awareness, and ritual knowledge. You cannot fight the entity — you must understand it well enough to survive it.

Rahasya is currently planned exclusively for PC via Steam. A planned release window is 2026. Console and other platform support may be considered post-launch depending on reception and resources.

A standard first playthrough is estimated at 2–3 hours. However, with randomized puzzle configurations and dynamic AI entity behavior, replayability is high — subsequent runs will present different layouts, challenges, and discoveries.

Rahasya draws thematic inspiration from Indian folklore, ancestral beliefs, ritual traditions, and the cultural significance of family havelis. Specific supernatural elements are original interpretations designed to honor rather than replicate specific mythologies.
